# The Echo Tower

*A cooperative two-view puzzle for 3 players, inspired by Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes.*
*System-agnostic — drop into any D&D edition.*

---

## 1. Overview

The party is split. One player — **the Walker** — is trapped in a cycle of three small stone rooms, accompanied by ghostly echoes of their own past actions. The other two — **the Readers** — are sealed in a circular chamber called **the Loom**, full of statues, candles, and cryptic murals that react to everything the Walker does.

Neither side can solve anything alone. The Walker can act but can't see the rules. The Readers can see the rules and the tower's state, but can't act in the tower — except in one crucial way: **they control where the Walker goes**.

The party communicates freely the whole time (sending stones, speaking tube — whatever fits your world). Constant chatter is the point.

**The three goals, in order:**

1. **Wake the Yellow Mime** (permanent unlock)
2. **Unlock the Mirror Room** (permanent unlock — impossible without Yellow)
3. **Solve the Mirror Room** (the finale — impossible on the first visit by design)

These two unlocks are the *only* permanent progress. Everything else in the tower is live, simultaneous state — nothing is banked, nothing accumulates. Random flailing produces nothing.

---

## 2. Setup at the Table

- Physically separate the Walker from the Readers if you can (different rooms, or a screen/headphones setup online). They may talk freely at all times but never see each other's information.
- The DM needs: **two index cards** labeled BLUE and YELLOW (for mime scripts), a route log, and the quick reference (section 11).
- Give the Readers paper. They *will* need notes. Do not tell them this; let them discover it.

## 3. Core Rules

### Stations and gestures

Every tower room shares the same footprint: a small stone chamber, **door on the south wall**, and interaction points at fixed positions: **N, E, W** (plus, in the Mirror Room only, **NW and NE**).

The Walker acts by declaring a **gesture at a position**: "I pull the lever on the east wall," "I reach through the north grate and grab at the air." Both count identically. A gesture at an empty or grated position does nothing *now* — but it is recorded.

### Scripts

Everything the Walker does in a room is logged, in order, as a list of positions — this is that room's **script**. Reaching at nothing is recorded exactly the same as pulling a real lever.

### The Mimes

- **Blue Mime** (present from the first door onward): each time the Walker passes a door, Blue's script is replaced with the script of the room *just left*. On entering a room, Blue silently replays its script, position by position.
- **Yellow Mime** (locked at start): once awakened, Yellow carries the script from **two rooms ago**. Each door passage shifts scripts down: Yellow takes Blue's old script, Blue takes the newest.

The Walker therefore carries at most a **two-room memory**, and every door passage scrolls it. A wrong door erases plans.

### The Hold rule (the heart of the puzzle)

Nothing in the tower responds to being *triggered*. Stations respond to being **held**: a lever held down, a plate stood on, a rope kept taut. All conditions in this puzzle are checked as **simultaneous states**.

- **A mime, after replaying its script, freezes holding the final position of that script** — and stays holding it until the Walker leaves the room.
- **The Walker holds whatever he is physically gripping or standing on right now.**
- Consequence: each mime can hold exactly **one** station — the last one in its script. Sloppy extra actions overwrite the hold.

### Grates

Some stations sit behind spectral iron grates. The Walker cannot reach them (his hand stops at the iron — record the gesture anyway). **Mimes pass through grates freely.** These stations can only ever be held by a mime.

## 4. The Tower Rooms

Three rooms cycle (plus the Mirror Room once unlocked). All identical in size; the objects differ.

| Room | N | E | W | NW / NE corners |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bell** | bell-rope *(grated)* | bronze lever | pressure plate | empty |
| **Flame** | iron poker in a brazier | iron lever *(grated)* | hanging chain | empty |
| **Chain** | chain-wheel *(grated)* | counterweight ring | pressure plate | empty |
| **Mirror** *(locked)* | silver lever | — | — | NW lever *(grated)* / NE lever *(grated)* |

All "held" flavors: lever held down, plate stood on, rope/chain/ring kept pulled taut, poker kept stirring the coals, wheel kept turned.

The NW and NE corners of the three normal rooms are **bare stone** — there is deliberately nothing there. (This matters for the finale.)

### Read-aloud, first room (Bell)

> You wake in a small stone chamber, cold and windowless. A single door of dark wood stands in the south wall. To the east, a bronze lever juts from the stone. To the west, a worn pressure plate is set into the floor. And to the north, behind a lattice of pale, frost-cold iron, hangs a bell-rope you cannot reach — your fingers stop dead against the grate, though you'd swear you can see through it like smoke.

### When the Walker first passes a door

> The next room is almost the same — almost. And you are not alone. A translucent, blue-tinged figure steps out of the wall behind you. It has your face. It begins, methodically, to do everything you just did in the last room — every pull, every touch, every grasp at empty air — and when it finishes, it stops, and stays, still holding its last gesture like a statue.

### The Mirror Room (once unlocked)

> A room of black glass. The entire north wall is a mirror, and set into it are three silver levers: one at the northwest corner, one dead center, one at the northeast corner. The outer two sit behind grates of that same cold iron. In the mirror you see yourself — and behind your reflection, faintly, a round room full of candlelight that is not the room you are standing in.

## 5. The Loom (the Readers' room)

A circular chamber with no exit. Its features, from the outside in:

### The outer ring — four statues

Four robed statues stand on a great rotating ring, each bearing a glyph: **a bell, a flame, a chain, a mirror**. Properties:

- The statue of the room the Walker currently occupies has **glowing eyes**. (The Mirror statue's eyes are shut until goal 2.)
- Each statue can be **rotated in place** by the Readers to face any direction.
- The whole outer ring **grinds one notch clockwise every time the Walker passes a door**. This is also the clock — see section 6.
- The Readers do *not* know which glyph is which room. Matching glyphs to the Walker's descriptions is their first job.

### The inner ring — the beam

A groove of white light runs from the center of the floor outward. The Readers can **drag the beam to point at any statue**. This is the routing mechanism (section 6). Nothing labels it; a mural verse is the only hint.

### The candle ring

Twelve candles on a central stone table — one per station in the tower (nine, plus the three Mirror levers, which stay dark until unlocked). A candle **burns while its station is held** and gutters out when released:

- **Orange flame** — held by the living (the Walker)
- **Blue flame** — held by the Blue Mime
- **Yellow flame** — held by the Yellow Mime

A brief touch makes a candle flicker for a moment. The candles are unlabeled — the Readers must build the map by correlation ("touch the east thing now" → "the third candle flickered orange!").

### The murals

Four arcs of verse circle the wall, one behind each statue. **An arc is legible only while its room is occupied by the Walker** — the rest lie in shadow. The Mirror arc stays dark until the Mirror Room is unlocked. Full text in section 8. This drip-feed forces note-taking and forces the Readers to *steer the Walker* to rooms whose rules they haven't read yet.

### Read-aloud, the Loom

> A round chamber, doorless, warm with candlelight. Four hooded statues stand on a ring of stone — bell, flame, chain, mirror carved at their breasts. One pair of stone eyes is glowing softly. Twelve candles stand on the table at the center, all cold and dark, and a single groove of white light runs across the floor like a compass needle. Around the wall, lines of verse — but only the arc behind the glowing-eyed statue catches the light well enough to read.

## 6. Routing and the Clock

**The Walker does not choose where he goes.** When he steps through a room's south door, he arrives at whichever room's statue **the beam is touching** at that moment.

- His hands program the mimes; his feet go where the Readers send him. Each side owns half of every plan.
- **The outer ring rotates one notch per door passage**, dragging the statues with it — so the beam is mis-aimed after *every single transit* and must be re-aimed before each exit. No set-and-forget. ("Don't step through yet — okay, beam's on the chain statue, *go*.")
- Beam pointing at nothing, or at the shut-eyed Mirror statue: the Walker's door loops back into the **same room**.
- A mis-aim isn't fatal, but it scrolls the two-room script memory — plans erased, start the choreography over.

**The clock:** the outer ring has 24 notch-marks. When it completes a full revolution (24 door passages), the candles gutter and the tower "finishes its turn" — consequence of your choice (a hunting shade enters the rooms, the Loom floods, or simply: both rooms reset and scripts wipe). **Rewind:** once per room-visit, the Readers may snuff and immediately relight the candle of the station the Walker is *currently holding* — the ring grinds back one notch. Only possible if their candle map is correct. Bookkeeping becomes a survival tool.

## 7. The Three Goals

### Goal 1 — Wake the Yellow Mime *(permanent)*

All of the following **simultaneously**:

1. **Blue Mime holds the chain-wheel** (N station, Chain room, grated) — i.e., the Walker's *final* gesture in the previous room was at position N;
2. **The Walker holds the pressure plate** (W station, Chain room);
3. **The three room-statues in the Loom are correctly aligned**: each rotated to face the statue of the room the Walker went to *immediately after* his most recent visit to that statue's room. (If he looped back into the same room, that statue faces the candle table at the center.)

When all three are true at once, a yellow flame kindles on the candle table, and in the tower a golden figure steps out of the Walker's shadow. **Permanent.**

*Why it needs both sides:* the Walker's half is a two-room combination he can't understand without the Chain arc verse (which the Readers read to him); the Readers' half requires reconstructing his route history from their notes on the glowing eyes, and holding the alignment at the exact moment he executes — live coordination, both directions.

### Goal 2 — Unlock the Mirror Room *(permanent)*

In any one room, **all three stations — N, E, and W — held in the same instant.**

One of the three is always grated, and each actor holds exactly one station, so this needs **three actors**: Yellow holding one (final gesture *two rooms ago*), Blue holding one (final gesture *one room ago*), the Walker holding the third *now*. Mathematically impossible before Goal 1.

It's a three-room choreography executed backward through the Walker's own history — while the Readers route him through the correct sequence of rooms with the self-scrambling beam. Both sides must be perfect for three consecutive doors.

On success: a **silver door** appears in the south-east corner of every room, permanently; the Mirror statue's eyes open; the Mirror candle-trio appears on the table. The silver door always leads to the Mirror Room and ignores the beam. Its south door leaves normally (beam-routed).

### Goal 3 — Solve the Mirror Room *(finale)*

**All three silver levers — NW, N, NE — held in the same instant.** NW and NE are grated.

The first visit fails by design, twice over: (a) the requirement is only learnable inside — and the Mirror mural arc only lights for the Readers during a Mirror visit; (b) the mimes' scripts on arrival are whatever produced the unlock — final positions N/E/W — and the levers sit at **NW and NE, positions that don't exist in the other rooms**. Nothing they've ever done touches them.

So the first visit is recon. Then the real plan: leave, and in the next two rooms deliberately end with a gesture **at the bare NW corner** (two rooms out) and **at the bare NE corner** (one room out) — grasping at empty stone, on faith — then take the silver door. Yellow arrives and freezes gripping the NW lever; Blue grips the NE; the Walker holds the center.

> The mirror does not shatter. It thins, like fog burning off — and behind it is a round room full of candlelight, four statues, and two very startled friends. The Loom was behind the glass all along.

Party reunited. Done.

## 8. The Mural Verses (Readers' handout, revealed arc by arc)

**Arc of the Bell** — *the law of shadows*

> *What the living does, the shadow does one door later.*
> *What the living did the door before, the golden shadow keeps.*
> *Each shadow rests where it last reached — and rests there holding.*
> *Cold iron stays the living; shadows pass, and grip.*

**Arc of the Flame** — *the light and the silver door*

> *The living walks where the light is willed.*
> *Hold one room whole — north, east, and west as one —*
> *and the tower will show its silver door.*

**Arc of the Chain** — *the second shadow*

> *When a shadow keeps the wheel,*
> *and the living keeps the stone,*
> *and each watcher faces where the living next walked —*
> *the second shadow wakes.*

**Arc of the Mirror** — *dark until Goal 2*

> *Three hands upon the north wall, held as one.*
> *Bring a shadow for each hand you lack,*
> *and let each arrive already holding.*

## 9. Verified Solution Walkthrough

One clean path, for the DM's confidence. (Any equivalent route works.)

**Phase 0 — discovery.** Walker wakes in Bell, describes it; Readers describe the Loom; Bell arc read aloud. Walker experiments; Readers correlate candles. First door passage: Blue Mime appears, outer ring lurches, and eventually someone drags the beam and the Walker exits somewhere unexpected — routing discovered.

**Phase 1 — Yellow.** Readers steer the Walker to any room; his **last** gesture there is a grasp at position N (real or empty — irrelevant). Readers re-aim the beam at the Chain statue; he exits. In Chain: Blue replays and freezes holding the chain-wheel (blue candle lights). Walker stands on the W plate (orange candle). Readers, who have been logging his route, set each statue to face where he next walked, and confirm alignment. **Yellow wakes.**

**Phase 2 — Mirror unlock.** Target: Bell room (N rope grated, E lever, W plate). Choreography: room A — final gesture at **N**; beam re-aimed; room B — final gesture at **E**; beam re-aimed at Bell; enter. Yellow replays room A's script → freezes holding the bell-rope (N). Blue replays room B's → freezes holding the bronze lever (E). Walker stands on the plate (W). Three candles burn as one. **Silver doors appear everywhere.**

**Phase 3 — recon.** Walker takes the silver door. Sees the three levers (NW/N/NE, outer two grated); Readers read the newly lit Mirror arc. His current scripts end at N and E — useless here, as designed. He leaves by the south door (beam-routed as normal).

**Phase 4 — finale.** Room A: final gesture a grasp at the **bare NW corner**. Beam re-aimed. Room B: final gesture at the **bare NE corner**. Silver door. Yellow grips NW, Blue grips NE, Walker holds center. **Mirror dissolves.**

Consistency checks: Goal 2 is impossible pre-Yellow (three stations, two holders); Goal 3 is impossible on first entry (arrival scripts end at N/E/W, never NW/NE); silver-door passage shifts scripts exactly like any door, which is what makes "arrive already holding" work.

## 10. DM Running Notes

- **Log scripts verbatim** on the BLUE and YELLOW cards, overwriting at each door. Early confused flailing gets replayed faithfully by mimes for two rooms — mostly harmless, occasionally hilarious, occasionally in the way. That's the recursion: the Walker's past mistakes share the room with him.
- **Mid-script actions are momentary** — only the frozen final hold matters for conditions. But narrate the mimes fully; watching your own past self methodically grope a blank wall is the flavor.
- **Narrate candles to the Readers unprompted** and eye-glow changes immediately — their information flow must feel live.
- **Hint ladder** if stuck: 1) a mural arc they haven't visited glints, prompting routing there; 2) a mime "lingers" meaningfully at its final hold; 3) the beam hums when touched.
- **Pacing:** 60–90 minutes. If faster is needed, shrink the clock or have an arc verse legible from the start.
- **Skill checks (optional):** none are required — this is a pure logic puzzle. If your table expects rolls, use them only for flavor reveals (Arcana to sense the grates are spectral, History to date the statues), never to bypass reasoning.
- **The trap to avoid:** never let the Walker's *own* room choice work. The beam decides. If players forget, the door simply opens back into the same room until the Readers aim.

## 11. Quick Reference (DM screen)

**Scripts:** Blue = last room. Yellow = two rooms ago. Every door: Yellow ← Blue ← newest.
**Hold:** mime freezes at *final* script position; Walker holds what he grips now. All checks simultaneous.
**Grates:** mimes only. **Routing:** beam's statue = destination; ring rotates 1 notch/door; 24 notches = clock out; rewind = snuff+relight the currently-held station's candle (1/visit).

**Goal 1 (Yellow):** Chain room — Blue holds N wheel + Walker holds W plate + statues face route-history. → permanent.
**Goal 2 (Silver door):** any room, N+E+W all held (Yellow + Blue + Walker). → permanent.
**Goal 3 (Finale):** Mirror room, NW+N+NE levers held (Yellow + Blue + Walker). NW/NE programmed at bare corners of prior rooms.

| Room | N | E | W |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bell | rope 🔒 | lever | plate |
| Flame | poker | lever 🔒 | chain |
| Chain | wheel 🔒 | ring | plate |
| Mirror | lever | — (NE lever 🔒) | — (NW lever 🔒) |

🔒 = grated (mimes only).
